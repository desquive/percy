I thought the code.org lessons in 3rd grade had ruined my chances of ever seriously programming. Funny how now it’s basically become my life as a third-year computer science student at USC. As a First Generation student born of Mexican parents, I am beyond grateful for the opportunity and blessing to study the great field of tech.

My main priorities these days aside from my studies are my positions as Director of Software Engineering for Trojan Tech Solutions, Student Ambassador and Community Life and Culture Chair at the USC Caruso Catholic Center, and my non-existent solo drumming career.

My main goals are to inspire young, black and hispanic students to pursue careers in Tech as well as emphasize the power of personality and a smile behind the computer screen.

When I myself am not behind said screen, you can find me at a soccer field, beach volleyball court, basketball court, or record store near you. Aside from playing sports and collecting music, I really enjoy playing the drums (whenever I can get my hands on a set) and spending time with my friends and family, especially my little baby nephew, doing anything imaginable.

Raspberry Pi Sound Recognition System


As part of a research project during my senior year of high school, this sound recognition system utilizes an open-source project found on Github that resembles a Shazam algorithm in order to recognize sound and songs. The scope of the linked research paper focuses on the use of the Raspberry Pi 4B device and the described program to provide assistance and accommodations to deaf or hard of hearing people in their own homes and the feasibility of its implementation.
